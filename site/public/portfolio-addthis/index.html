AddThis Apps

Building widgets and Facebook apps

Background

AddThis was originally a widget platform called Clearspring, and I was part of their internal creative studio. I built out custom widgets for a variety of media clients.

As Senior Web Developer at AddThis/Clearspring, I handled each client's project from conception to launch with deadlines ranging from one week to a couple months.

Micro Experiences

Each project was focused around a single user experience. My job was to come up with the best way to deliver a high quality custom experience and make the content as shareable as possible.

For each project, I would:

  • Receive an RFP and lead a project kickoff with the client to brainstorm ideas and provide insights into best practices for sharable content
  • Design wireframes
  • Create and finalize the designs
  • Develop the widget or app in Flash or HTML/CSS/JS
  • Launch the app on our platform, and provide client with shareable embed code

Unique Challenges

Since every project was a stand-alone experience, each one required creative problem solving.

Some of the higlights of my work were:

  • A SpongeBob Squarepants Facebook mood sharing app that leveraged the Facebook API to determine which moods were most popular and which were trending with your friends
  • A Lego Indiana Jones Flash app where you could mix and match body parts to dynamically generate an avatar image that could be shared in your timeline.
  • A suite of over 50 ESPN widgets that leveraged their data APIs so you could track your teams and sports in real time on your Facebook page
